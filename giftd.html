Project Overview

Context

Giftd is an app, available on iOS and android, through which you can gift your pre-loved clothes to friends, family and neighbors. More than 3600 downloads and over 2000 gifts have been made since it started in November 2021.

Take a look - www.giftd.app

Timeline

July 2022 - ongoing

Team

1 UX/UI designer - Me, 1 founder, 1 co founder and CTO

Responsibilities

My involvement with Giftd started in July and it is ongoing, since then I have been taking on a couple of challenges.

As the sole UX/UI designer I am responsible for ​​improving existing flows, working on new features, initial discovery and iterate design solutions at all levels of fidelity. In this case study I will talk about how I approached challenges such as:

01. Optimizing and iterating on the onboarding flow

02. Designing a filtering option

Improve the app experience and impact the number of people joining and making clothes transactions.

Since joining Giftd as a volunteer, I had three key personal goals that I wanted to learn and improve on:

  • Put into practice what I have learned and learn how to articulate and inform my design decisions

  • Learning to be adaptable with the workflow

Onboarding

It all started with... Finding an opportunity for improving the onboarding experience

Giftd is a product that listens to its users feedback, and so for my first meeting with the founder I decided to bring my experience, as a user, as I was going through the onboarding flow.

According to the data 90% of users succeed in the process with just 10% abandoning, and users generally find it to be easy and fast. What the current onboarding is doing is the set up moment, creating an account, granting permission, asking for personal information, these actions are necessary however they don’t provide immediate benefits.

What I noticed that was missing from the current onboarding was communicating the value that I get as a user from using Giftd. Since the product is constantly evolving, together with the founder we examined the present flow to seek for opportunities and eliminate stages that are not essential. Here is what we found:

01. Opportunity: How can we communicate the value of the product?

02. Opportunity / Problem: Users don’t know exactly what they are supposed to upload

03. Problem: As a new user joining without invite vs. user joining via invite, the flow looks different and is confusing

Goals and Objectives

How might we allow users to easily connect with their friends and family

How might we communicate to users the value they get out of Giftd

How might we encourage users to post their first item during onboarding

Research

Doing my personal research

I conducted a competitive analysis that helped me to look into strategic insights, flows and design solutions of competitor’s onboarding. The main objective of the competitive analysis was to look into how users connect with friends and family during onboarding.

When considering how to convey the value of Giftd, reading about best practices for onboarding as well as reading insights from marketing on how to communicate to the generation of our users, was very helpful.

As I was doing the research, a survey was sent out to the users to identify pain points, motivations and overall experience. I then collected and identified the most common pain points, requests and areas of improvement. In regards to the onboarding flow, users didn’t express any pain points and the typical requirement is for it to be quick with a complete setup.

Ideating & Sketching

What would be the impact on the product if we don’t improve the onboarding?

During the brainstorm session with the founder, I started the meeting with questions. Having the questions answered will help us make better decisions, ensuring that we are aligned on what are the measurable key performance indicators and what are the business and user goals in regards to the new solution. In addition we have also reviewed the current flow.

What are the users needs during onboarding?

What is the main problem we’re trying to solve?

How are we going to measure the solution?

After some initial ideas and mock-ups were created, together with the founder and the developer, we decided for this MVP to concentrate on allowing users to easily add their contacts as we hope that this will maximize user generated user growth.

As a second priority for improving the onboarding, we will allow users to upload items within the onboarding flow. The desired outcome for this solution is to increase conversion of uploads. Additionally we will also make the onboarding more friendly by adding photos and explaining the principles of Gifted in 4 easy steps. This flow is currently in progress.

Potential future vision

Since the founder is already introducing product updates via video on Instagram, I proposed as a potential future solution for the onboarding to be a video.

Users coming for Instagram feel continuity and familiarity. With video as a user you immediately land into an engaging experience, instead of a button as a CTA​​ after the user is given an overview of the app and its benefits at the end of the video, they may be asked to complete a task to encourage them to try the product by themselves.

Impact

The syncing contacts have been available since August and it is too soon to have data on it, however we do see growth and engagement which is a good sign.

Learnings

In terms of design suggestions it was important to make sure that I understood the constraints and suggest solutions that are less time consuming and easy to implement.

Filters

Implementing the first filters and running into problems

One of the main pain points I found when gathering the survey's data was that there was no filtering option, which made it difficult for users to find items that were available, local, or published by friends.

Together with the founder and the developer, we decided to start by allowing users to filter by friends, reserved, public and gifted items and expand on the next MVP.

I was excited to design the filtering options and have users use it, but after only one week users began to contact us with concerns: "I think there is a bug since I can't see anything when I am filtering."

When designing the mockups, I failed to take user journeys and flows into account. I didn't think about things like: what would the user see if they tried to filter just by friends but didn't have any? With the current solution users will be encountered by an empty screen with no visual guidance, which causes confusion.

As a potential solution I would have added text to the empty screen directing the user back to the filtering options. The text would be written in a friendly tone, indicating why the screen is blank and what to do next.

Learnings

What this experience taught me is that when the design gets into the real world there will always be something to learn from that we won't know about until users start using it.
