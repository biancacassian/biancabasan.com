Project Overview

Context

Giftd is a Berlin-based start-up and social network app that aims to eradicate clothing waste by enabling users to gift their pre-loved clothes to loved ones and neighbors. This fosters a tight-knit community while promoting eco-friendly habits.

As the excitement around Giftd continues to grow, we now face an exciting challenge: How can we design a solution that seamlessly connects users with Giftd mission while recognizing and celebrating the top 3-4% of our most engaged users, the true champions driving change on the platform?

Timeline

February - Shipped in March 2023

Team

1 UX/UI Designer - Me, 1 Founder, 1 Co Founder and CTO

2 Marketing Team

UX Goals

1. Connect Users to Giftd's Mission: Ensure that the feature establishes a strong connection between users and Giftd's mission, communicating the platform's purpose and users impact clearly and effectively.

2. Recognize Top Engaged Users: Identify and celebrate the top engaged users who have made the most significant impact on Giftd's platform, providing them with special recognition for their contributions.

3. Enhance User Engagement: Create a feature that encourages users to actively participate in Giftd's platform, fostering a sense of involvement and interaction.

Role

As the sole designer, I took the lead in driving the entire project from its initial idea to the final hand-off. Working closely with the product owner, developer and marketing I facilitated alignment and feature discovery workshop using different methodologies. As well as staying closely connected with users through thorough research.

Discover the Issue

How can we celebrate the top 3-4% of engaged users with the greatest impact?

To kick start the design process, I first looked into how other companies approach impact, particularly looking at how they communicate numbers and encourage engagement.

Simultaneously, I discussed with the founder to define the feature's purpose and the and the value it would bring to users. The goal emerged: to boost engagement for primary and secondary users, celebrating the top 3-4% engaged users making the greatest impact.

Using the Hook Model framework to brainstorm about the habit we aim to enhance

I took the lead in exploring the psychology of motivation and engagement, which eventually led me to discover the Hook Model— a framework for creating habit-forming products in four steps.

Excited about the framework, I led a brainstorming session with the founder and developer, using the Hook Model to generate feature ideas. We concentrated on identifying desired rewards for primary users and designing to fulfill those needs, all while ensuring these rewards foster user engagement and growth.

From the brainstorming session we gained 3 valuable insights:

01. Gamification elements can be used to enhance engagement

02. For gamification to work we need to uncover the real motivation / goal of the user

03. We must experiment with creating a loop of triggered actions and unpredictable rewards for users

GIFTD Super Users

“I want to see my impact on contributing to the growing community of gifting”

Through our use of the Hook Model and previous user research, we developed 3 assumptions about what is important to users in terms of seeing progress and the impact they want to have as ambassadors.

Although these assumptions were based on data from previous research, we decided to validate them with our primary users before beginning the design process.

What we’ve learned by reaching out to +20 Giftd ambassadors:

90%

Our users are most incentivized by their environmental impact

Our users want to see the wider community impact they have by inviting others and growing their community

10%

Our users are less interested in seeing how friends of friends are making an impact

Joining Forces

"How can we emotionally connect users to their CO2e savings through the user interface?"

Through engaging with Giftd ambassadors, It became evident that integrating a referral option and emphasizing the users' environmental impact as they engage in the clothing gifting process would be a game-changer.

During ideation our goal was to align with users' sustainability aspirations, transforming mere numbers into a compelling reflection of their impact, motivating and empowering them on their sustainability journey.

Design explorations

Introducing Community Impact

How can design and psychology be used in a progress bar?

The challenged that we faced was to design a solution that will enhance user engagement for both primary and secondary users while celebrating the top engaged users with the greatest impact. Let's dive into the outcomes:

Beyond showcasing top users with badges, we introduced a progress bar. This bar not only lets users monitor their personal and community progress but also fuels their motivation to minimize CO2e emissions. It's a visual testament to their impactful journey and offers a glimpse into their friends' contributions through gifting.

Short term rewards

Leveraging the Goal Gradient Effect, which emphasizes the power of small rewards, I divided the progress bar into milestones. This approach celebrates users as they conquer each milestone, adding an exciting sense of achievement to their journey.

Community Impact

The Community Impact Screen is where users can track their own progress and that of their community towards CO2e reduction, while also offering invitations that grant one month of free reservations.

The Community Impact screen also serve as an entry-point for an action, such as “ Invite more people”.

Metaphors vs. numbers or “People remember stories better than facts alone”

We aimed to make the experience of observing and monitoring your impact enjoyable, so we came up with the idea to use an empty human icon that gradually fills up as you make progress

With a progress bar, are we encouraging bad behavior? And if yes, how can we prevent it?

While designing the progress bar, I also considered its potential downsides and wondered how I could prevent negative emotions and unethical behavior.

01. Transparent: communicate what the bar represents and what it is measuring and how

02. Fake progress bar: make sure that you’re tracking in real time

03. Provide clear feedback to users when a process is complete or if there is an error

04. Accessible: use high contrast colors to make it easier for users to see

Impact

1. My active role unfolded through cross-functional workshops, which united the team for collaborative brainstorming sessions and I have also enhanced the workflow between the developer and myself for more efficient collaboration.

2. Users started expressing their delight in the newly introduced feature. Their positive feedback highlighted the success of our efforts in creating an engaging and meaningful user experience.

Challenges

The main obstacle arose when addressing the user journey for individuals who were joining through referrals. Given the already comprehensive onboarding process, introducing an extra screen exclusively for the referral code raised concerns about potential user experience challenges and drop-offs. Post-launch, we recognized that our initial solution for the referral code lacked prominence, prompting us to reevaluate our approach.

Reflecting on this journey

Not to be precious with my first ideas, we went through a couple of iterations, and I really appreciated how through different viewpoints and feedback, we arrived at the best design solution for the launch.

Even when assumptions seem solid, the real value comes from validating them with our users.

Through this project I've come to understand the importance of testing designs on real devices, particularly actual phones, before finalizing the product.
